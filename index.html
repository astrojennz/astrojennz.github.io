Research Projects & Publications

Detecting exoplanet atmospheres in the presence of stellar activity

In 2023, I began my PhD in Astrophysics under the supervision of Nuno Santos and Olivier Demangeon. My research focuses on understanding how stellar activity affects high-resolution transmission spectroscopy. So far, I have developed simulations of a transiting hot Jupiter orbiting a Sun-like star and analysed how the size and position of unocculted starspots impact the observed absorption spectra. Next, I will use the Paranal Solar ESPRESSO Telescope (PoET) to obtain high-resolution spectra of stellar spots. These data will be incorporated into the models to improve the simulations and study this effect using real observations...

Revision of the ESPRESSO calibration plan

In 2023 had the opportunity to participate in the ESO Summer Students Chile 2023 Program, which is being held at the European Southern Observatory facilities in Chile. In this opportunity, I worked under the supervision of Dr. Matias Jones in order to be able to answer this question. For this, I reduced and analyzed calibration data from the ESPRESSO spectrograph on the VLT, and determined the adequate validity periods for each type of calibration, based on the stability requirements of the instrument. The purpose of this project is to determine how old a calibration can be used to produce high-quality reduced data that will be later used to search for Earth-like planets...

Exoplanet detection with CHIRON spectrograph

In 2021 I worked under the supervision of Dr. James Jenkins, in the development of an algorithm to obtain radial velocities (RVs) with data from the CHIRON spectrograph, located at the CTIO/SMARTS 1.5 m telescope. I developed an algorithm that uses the cross-correlation method with a noise-free binary mask, in order to confirm and characterize exoplanet candidates from TESS. Through this experience, I was able to work closely with high-resolution echelle data of the star Tau Ceti, which was used to test my algorithm. In the process, I became familiar with the systematics of the instrument, and the requirements for measuring precision radial velocities...
