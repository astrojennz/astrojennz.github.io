About Me

My name is Jennifer, but everyone calls me Jenn. I was born and raised in Mexico City. At 20, I moved to Santiago, Chile (yes, to the opposite side of the continent) to pursue my undergraduate studies in Astronomy at Pontificia Universidad Cat√≥lica de Chile. During my time in college, I had the chance to explore different areas of research, from studying active galaxies using MUSE data to searching for exoplanets with ground-based telescopes in Chile.

I am currently based in Porto, Portugal, pursuing a PhD in Astrophysics. My research focuses on how stellar activity, particularly spots, affects the high-resolution transmission spectra of giant and Earth-like planets orbiting Sun-like stars. As part of this work, I am a member of the PoET Science Team, connected to the ESPRESSO spectrograph through a solar telescope.
